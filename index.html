About me

Technical, professional, educational, and volunteer experiences


Certifications CompTIA

I obtained the Computing Technology Industry Association (CompTIA)'s A+, Network+, and Security+ certifications in July 2018 to validate entry-level security knowledge I obtained during my freshman year at RIT through classes, clubs, and security competitions.

I plan to obtain the Cisco Certified Networking Associate (CCNA) certification in Routing & Switching or the CompTIA PenTest+ certification during RIT's 2018-19 winter intercession. It is my goal to eventually prepare myself to pursue certifications such as OSCP and CISSP later during my studies at RIT.


Awards ACT, NFIB

I achieved Exemplar status during the American College Test's 2017 College and Career Readiness Campaign due to exam performance and my efforts to bring science, technology, engineering, and math educational opportunities to my high school in rural northern Minnesota.

I was also awarded the National Federation of Independent Businesses' Young Entrepreneur Award for Minnesota for founding Tauber IT Services to offer IT configuration and electronics repair services to small businesses in my community.


Activism FIRST, Sch. America

I was selected to attend Scholarship America's 2018 National Dreams to Success Awards for bringing the resources and opportunities to make STEM a viable career path to those in my rural community. Here, I met with White House officials on education, policy, and economics to discuss the future of STEM education and funding in public schools.

I also routinely volunteer for For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology (FIRST) regionals, events, and mentoring opportunities in the Rochester area. I hope to complete FIRST Robotics Competition Control System Advisor (CSA) training in March 2019.

Hello, I am Wyatt.

Computing Security student at Rochester Institute of Technology

I am currently a second-year honors computing security (CSEC) student at the Rochester Institute of Technology in Rochester, New York. I am passionate about security topics and hope to join the accelerated Bachelor of Science/Master of Science degree program as well as specialize in RIT's digital forensics and malware cluster when I enter my third year.

When I have the opportunity, I am active in clubs on campus such as the RIT Security Club (RITSEC, formerly RC3/SPARSA) and RIT FIRST. I also enjoy geocaching in the Rochester area and maintaing my caches at RIT, as well as discussing philosophy.
