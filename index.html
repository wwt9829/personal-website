Hello, I am Wyatt.

Computing Security student at Rochester Institute of Technology

I am a second-year honors computing security (CSEC) student at the Rochester Institute of Technology in Rochester, New York. I am passionate about security topics and am developing a career in the information technology and security industries. Some of my interests are malware research and reversing (which I hope to specialize in later in my career), social engineering, encryption, and steganography. I also volunteer my time creating STEM education and employment opportunities for students in rural areas.

When I have the opportunity, I am active in clubs on campus such as the RIT Security Club (RITSEC, formerly RC3/SPARSA) and RIT FIRST. I also enjoy geocaching in the Rochester area, discussing philosophy, and sailing.

Accomplishments

Technical, professional, educational, and volunteer involvements


Awards ACT, NFIB

I was one of four students to achieve Exemplar status during the ACT's 2017 College and Career Readiness Campaign due to exam performance and my efforts to bring science, technology, engineering, and math educational opportunities to my high school in rural northern Minnesota.

I was also awarded the National Federation of Independent Business's Young Entrepreneur Award for Minnesota for founding Tauber IT Solutions to offer IT configuration and electronics repair services to small businesses in my community. I was one of 100 students to receive this award nationally, and the only student in Minnesota to receive the award in 2017.


Certifications Cisco, CompTIA

I currently hold CompTIA Security+ and Cisco Certified Entry Networking Technician (CCENT) status, as well as CompTIA Network+ and A+. Certifications are complementary to my classes at RIT, and I see them as an excellent way to validate and reinforce my growing skill set. It is my goal to eventually pursue industry-leading certifications such as OSCP and CISSP later in my career.

I plan to obtain further certifications in networking and blue team operations while on co-op during summer 2019. These certifications will validate skills learned in my systems administration, computer forensics, and computer malware classes.


Activism

FIRST, Scholarship America

I was one of 30 honorees at Scholarship America's 2018 National Dreams to Success Awards for bringing the resources and opportunities to make STEM a viable career path to those in my rural community. Here, I met with White House officials on education, policy, and economics to discuss the future of STEM education and funding in public schools.

I also routinely volunteer for For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology (FIRST) regionals, events, and mentoring opportunities in the Rochester area, and help high school students get involved with the security community as a Computing Security ambassador for RIT.

RIT Security Club (RITSEC)

Current | 3rd, 4th Place

I participate in and create weekly CTF challenge solutions for RITSEC Weekly CTF, a semester-long Capture the Flag challenge by the RIT Security Club. My goal is for my solutions to give insight into the strategy and reasoning of a CTF.
