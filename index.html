Hello! I am Wyatt.

Cybersecurity student at the Rochester Institute of Technology

I am a third-year student in the M.S. Computing Security program at RIT, just starting my career in the industry and striving to create technology that is secure, inclusive and accessible for all people, and a joy to use. Some of the ways I'm achieving my goal are by researching computer malware, forensics, and usable security, participating in cybersecurity red/blue competitions and Capture the Flag events, traveling for STEM outreach around the world, and working to to address bias and data ethics in academic research and the media. I also enjoy being a lifelong student through incorporating other disciplines and perspectives into my activities, from technology to psychology, philosophy, journalism, communication, and politics.

On campus, I am a member of the RIT Security Club (RITSEC), RIT FIRST, and the RIT Honors Program. I also enjoy adventurous activities such as geocaching in the Rochester area, climbing, and traveling with my friends. In the future, I hope to obtain a Ph.D. in information assurance and own a paramotor.

Accomplishments

Technical, professional, educational, and volunteer involvements



Awards NSF, ACT, NFIB

I was awarded the National Science Foundation's CyberCorps Scholarship for Service while attending RIT in recognition of my academic achievements and personal commitment to making national systems and data more secure and accountable to civilians.

I was also one of four students to achieve Exemplar status during the ACT's College and Career Readiness Campaign and one of 100 students to receive the National Federation of Independent Business's Young Entrepreneur Award. I received these awards for my continued efforts to bring STEM opportunities to my high school and surrounding communities in rural northern Minnesota.


Certifications CompTIA, Cisco

I currently hold CompTIA Cybersecurity Analyst+ (CySA+) and Cisco Certified Network Associate Security (CCNA Sec), as well as CompTIA PenTest+, Security+, and CCNA Routing and Switching (CCNA R&S). Certifications are complementary to my classes and projects at RIT, and I see them as an excellent way to validate and reinforce my growing skill set.

I plan to obtain further certifications in network security soon, as well as continue pursing certifications in system forensics and penetration testing. These certifications will validate skills I have learned in classes, while on internships, and during competitions.

I also routinely volunteer for For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology (FIRST) events, help high school students get involved with the security community as a Computing Security ambassador for RIT, and work with first-generation STEM students through RIT IMPRESS.
